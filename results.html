"Decision Making, Emotion Recognition and Childhood Traumatic Experiences in Murder Convicts Imprisoned with Aggravated Life Sentence: A Prison Study"



Results of the study

The study found that while both groups were similar in age and education, the antisocial/psychopathic group had more severe criminal backgrounds, higher exposure to physical abuse, greater emotional regulation issues, and significantly worse decision-making on behavioral tasks.

No major demographic differences between groups:

  • Age, marital status, and education were similar (p > 0.05)

Significant group differences found in:

  • Time spent in prison, type/severity of crime, and substance use

Antisocial/psychopathic group:

  • Higher rates of substance abuse (p < 0.05)
  • Specifically: mixed drug use (52.5%) and cannabis use (17.4%)
  • Military service status also differed significantly


Results from tests

On defense styles (DSQ)

Antisocial group scored worse across

  • Mature defenses(p = 0.003)
  • Neurotic defenses (p = 0.012)
  • Primitive defenses (p = 0.001)

Childhood trauma (CTQ)

  • Only physical abuse was higher in the antisocial group (p = 0.009)
  • No significant differences in emotional abuse, sexual abuse, neglect or total scores

Alexithymia (TAS-20)

  • Higher in the antisocial group (p = 0.029)

Psychopathy scores (PCL-R):

  • Antisocial group scored significantly higher in:
    • Interpersonal/affective traits
    • Social deviance
    • Total score (all p = 0.001)

No group differences in:

  • IQ Scores
  • RMET scores (emotion recognition from eyes), across all categories: male, female, positive, negative, neutral (p > 0.05)

Iowa Gambling Task results:

  • Antisocial group scored lower in:
    • 1st block (p = 0.008)
    • 2nd block (p = 0.032)
    • Total earnings (p = 0.009)
    • Debt score (p = 0.001)

PCL-R scores were positively correlated with:

  • Childhood physical trauma
  • Alexithymia (TAS-20)
  • Defense styles (DSQ)
  • But not with RMET performance

Some gleaning

The results show that the antisocial group displayed more severe emotional and behavioral problems despite the fact that two groups were comparable in age, education and marital status. The group were more likely to have a history of substance abuse, childhood physical abuse and unhealthy ways of coping.

Their test results displayed more difficulty in expressing feelings and unhealthy ways of managing emotions. They performed worse on decision-making tasks even though their intelligence and ability to recognize emotions were about the same as the control group. The findings show that early life experiences and emotional issues may be factors in later impulsive and destructive behaviors.
